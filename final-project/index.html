What is Facility Management?

Facilities Management (FM) is a profession focused on ensuring the functionality, comfort, safety, sustainability, and efficiency of the built environment. This includes the buildings we live and work in, along with their surrounding infrastructure. FM integrates people, places, and processes to improve quality of life and business productivity, ensuring seamless operations from the parking lot to the executive suite. Facility managers coordinate these processes to create a safe and efficient environment.

(Source: ifma.org)

Why is Facility Management relevant?

Facilities Management (FM) is a rapidly growing industry, especially highlighted during the COVID-19 pandemic when safety and health became top priorities. FM ensures the functionality, comfort, safety, sustainability, and efficiency of the buildings and infrastructure where we live, work, and play. Facility managers are crucial to organizations, contributing to strategy and the bottom line by ensuring operational efficiencies, planning infrastructure, managing risks, promoting sustainability, leveraging technology, mitigating natural disasters, guaranteeing compliance, and enhancing security.

(Source: ifma.org)
